Do You Really Need an AI Agent?

March 26, 2024
Reading time: 5 minutes

When I started planning my first commercially viable app, I was drawn into the promise of AI Agents. It sounded like an amazing shortcut that could save me time and more importantly, money: intelligent software that could automate complex tasks and make decisions on its own.

But I quickly ran into a key question: do I really need autonomy, or just automation? But before I could answer my own question, I had to understand what exactly is an AI Agent.

What is an AI Agent?

An AI agent is a software system that can:

  • Make autonomous decisions based on its environment
  • Learn from past interactions and adapt its behavior
  • Take independent actions to achieve specified goals
  • Handle complex, open-ended tasks without constant human guidance
  • Examples: chatbots, self-driving cars, personal assistants

Think of it like hiring a virtual assistant who can make decisions on their own, rather than following a strict set of instructions. While this sounds appealing, it's not always the best solution for every automation need.

What is an AI Workflow?

Unlike agents, an AI workflow is a structured sequence that:

  • Follows a predetermined path with clear decision points
  • Uses AI components for specific, well-defined tasks
  • Maintains predictable behavior patterns
  • Offers clear monitoring and debugging capabilities
  • Examples: data preprocessing, model training, testing automation

AI-powered workflows can inherit some unpredictability from their components, especially if they rely on non-deterministic models like GPT. But the overall process is structured and repeatable.

How About Both?

AI agents working together in workflows can handle complex tasks by breaking them down into manageable steps, with each agent specializing in a particular area, and collaborating to achieve a common goal.

    Example: Customer Service Automation

    Scenario: A customer service AI agent can handle routine inquiries and escalate complex issues to human agents with relevant context.

    Workflow:

    1. Agent 1 (Initial Query): Uses NLP to understand the customer's query
    2. Agent 2 (Knowledge Base): Retrieves relevant information if query is known
    3. Agent 3 (Escalation): Routes complex queries to human agents with context

    Think of it like a smart assembly line - each station knows exactly what to do, when to do it, and how to handle exceptions.

    Understanding the Confusion

    After watching a dozen “build your own agent” videos on YouTube and reading docs from OpenAI and Anthropic, I realized what I actually needed wasn't an AI Agent, I needed an AI Worflow.

    Feature AI Workflow AI Agent
    Decision Making Based on rules Autonomous
    Predictability High Variable
    Cost Control Predictable Can vary widely
    Best For Structured tasks Open-ended problems

    Key distinctions:

    • Workflow: Predefined steps for repeatable, predictable tasks
    • Agent: Autonomous decision-making to pursue open-ended goals

    Define Your Use Case

    When deciding between an AI agent and an AI workflow, ask yourself:

    • Is the task structured or open-ended?
    • Do I need real-time decision-making?
    • How much autonomy do I want the system to have?

    For example, for my app I needed:

    • Data fetching and transformation
    • Testing and debugging
    • Basic customer support automation

    These are structured, repetitive needs. Workflows are the right fit.

    (I also needed a way to monitor and adjust the workflow as needed, which is easier with workflows than agents.)

    Lessons Learned

    • Agents are powerful but unpredictable. They offer autonomy at the cost of predictability
    • Workflows are easier to manage, scale and debug, especially for well-defined tasks
    • Be aware of the unpredictability of LLMs within your workflows

    So, do you really need an AI Agent?

    It depends on your use case. If you need structured, predictable automation, an AI workflow is likely the better choice. But if you need a system that can adapt to new situations and make decisions on its own, an AI agent might be the way to go.

    Choose deliberately, do more research and build accordingly.
