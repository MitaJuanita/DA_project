Do You Really Need an AI Agent?

March 26, 2024

When I started planning my healthcare app, I got pulled into the "AI agent" hype vortex. It sounded amazing: intelligent software that could handle decision-making and automate complex tasks end-to-end.

But the deeper I got, the more I realized that what I needed wasn’t an agent—it was a workflow.

The Confusion: Agents vs. Workflows

After watching a dozen "how to build an agent" videos and reading documentation from OpenAI and Anthropic, I noticed most of what people called "agents" were actually structured sequences of tasks—i.e., workflows.

Here’s how I break it down:

  • AI Workflows: Predefined steps using AI tools to automate predictable, repeatable processes.
  • AI Agents: Autonomous systems that decide on steps dynamically to pursue a goal.

If your task can be mapped out in advance, you probably don’t need an agent. Just a smart workflow.

How I Decided What I Needed

My app needed help with:

  • Data fetching and transformation
  • Testing and debugging
  • Basic support ticket automation

None of those require autonomous reasoning. They require reliability, consistency, and speed—perfect for workflows.

Lessons Learned

  • Agents are powerful, but risky and harder to control.
  • Workflows are easier to debug, deploy, and trust.
  • Use agents only when flexibility and autonomy are required.

Buzzwords don’t solve problems—architecture does. Know what your problem is before picking a solution that sounds fancy.
