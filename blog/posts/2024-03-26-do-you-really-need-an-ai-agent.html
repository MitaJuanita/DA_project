Do You Really Need an AI Agent?

March 26, 2024

When I started planning my first commercially viable app, I got pulled into the "AI agent" hype vortex. It sounded amazing: intelligent software that could automate complex tasks and make decisions on its own.

But the deeper I went, the more I realized that what I actually needed wasn’t an agent. I needed a workflow.

Understanding the Confusion

After watching a dozen “build your own agent” videos and reading docs from OpenAI and Anthropic, I noticed something: most “agents” were just structured task flows—workflows with a fancy name.

flowchart LR subgraph Workflow [AI Workflow] Input([Input Data]) Step1([Data Preprocessing]) Step2([Model Training]) Output([Predictions]) Input --> Step1 --> Step2 --> Output end subgraph Agent [AI Agent Decision Process] Goal([Define Goal]) Decision{Choose Action} ActionA([Action A]) ActionB([Action B]) Result([Evaluate Result]) Goal --> Decision Decision --> ActionA --> Result Decision --> ActionB --> Result end

Key distinction:

  • Workflow: Predefined steps for repeatable, predictable tasks
  • Agent: Autonomous decision-making to pursue open-ended goals

My Use Case

  • Data fetching and transformation
  • Testing and debugging
  • Basic customer support automation

These are structured, repetitive needs. Workflows are the right fit.

Lessons Learned

  • Agents are powerful but unpredictable
  • Workflows are easier to manage and debug
  • Only use agents when real autonomy is required

Buzzwords don't solve problems. Architecture does.
